Generative AI is a type of artificial intelligence tool that generates images, text, videos, and other media from user prompts. It creates new content by learning patterns from vast datasets of existing documents and artifacts online.

Generative AI models use neural networks to identify patterns in existing data and generate new content through unsupervised and semi-supervised learning approaches. Foundation models are built from large unlabeled datasets, enabling predictions and creations like text, images or videos based on user inputs.

Examples of GenAI tools include GPT-4 for advanced text and multimodal tasks, ChatGPT for conversational content generation, and ElevenLabs for speech generation. Other notable ones are SORA for image and video creation from text prompts and Gemini for handling text, images, and more.

Generative AI accelerates research by analyzing complex data, enhances customer experiences with natural conversations, and optimizes business processes across engineering and marketing. It also boosts employees productivity through code/text suggestions, reports, and creative prototypes.

Generative AI outputs can be biased from training data, inconsistent across similar inputs, and inaccurate through hallucinations that spread misinformation. Additional risks include deepfakes for fraud, generation of sexually explicit content, and copyright violations from unlicensed data use.
