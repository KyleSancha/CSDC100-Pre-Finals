Ethical Use of AI

Ethical use of AI involves promoting fairness, accountability, and respect for human dignity in all stages of AI development and deployment. It also includes protecting the environment, ensuring inclusiveness, and supporting sustainable development so that AI contributes positively to society.

Responsible Use in the Academe

AI in the academe must be used responsibly so that it supports learning and preserves academic integrity. Schools and universities should put in place clear guidelines, promote AI literacy, and ensure human oversight so AI enhances education rather than undermines it.

Avoiding Plagiarism and Misuse

Students should avoid plagiarism by verifying AI-generated citations manually, cross-checking sources with academic databases, and ensuring original work when using AI tools.

Accuracy and Verification of AI Outputs

Responsible use of AI in academia also demands accuracy and verification of AI outputs. According to Clarivate, any generative-AI result used for research or learning must be assessed for relevance, faithfulness to sources, clarity, and completeness, ensuring outputs are reliable and appropriate for academic purposes.

Bias and Fairness

AI fairness aims to treat all groups equally by mitigating bias through diverse training data, bias detection techniques, and transparent decision-making to promote inclusive and equitable AI systems.
